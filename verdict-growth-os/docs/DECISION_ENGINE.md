# Verdict Growth OS — The Decision Engine

**Status:** **FROZEN operating-model candidate**, pre-Phase-2. This document is the
contract the Phase 2 build follows. It supersedes the "nine modules" framing in the
original `ARCHITECTURE.md`; those modules survive as *views over one ledger*, not as
independent systems. Extended by ADR-004 (reversibility), ADR-005 (decision
classes), ADR-006 (learning value + multi-constraint portfolio) — see §9–§12. The
recommendation to freeze is in §13.

---

## 0. One sentence

Verdict Growth OS is an **autonomous growth company** with **one objective**
(grow WatchVerdict and ReadVerdict), **one currency** (expected incremental
dollars, ΔEV), **one allocator** (the CEO function), a thin **executive
accountability layer**, and a **Growth Science loop** that recalibrates the
value model after every measured result.

The whole system exists to answer two questions as fast as possible:

1. **Where are WatchVerdict and ReadVerdict losing the most company value?**
2. **What is the cheapest credible way to acquire the next qualified, retained user?**

---

## 1. The pipeline

```
Observe → Normalize → Decision Engine → Prioritize → Recommend
        → Human Approval (when required) → Execute → Measure → Learn
```

- **Observe** — every department is a *sensor*; it emits typed Observations. It never prioritizes.
- **Normalize** — Observations become Signals: deduped, enriched, bound to a KPI-graph node, provenance attached.
- **Decision Engine** — Reasoners turn Signals into Decision Candidates (drawing from a data-driven Playbook); the ROI model prices each in dollars.
- **Prioritize** — a constrained portfolio selection (not a sort) picks the working set under budget, capacity, risk, dependencies, funnel-diversification, and an explore quota.
- **Recommend** — the ranked **Decision Ledger**; the top is the CEO morning brief.
- **Approval** — externally-visible / financial / deployment / pricing / outreach actions are default-denied until a human decides. No recommendation silently becomes an executable action.
- **Execute** — through an adapter/executor; idempotent; audited.
- **Measure** — actual KPI movement vs predicted, per decision.
- **Learn** — Growth Science attributes outcomes and updates the value graph, LTV, play base-rates, and executive credibility. The ledger re-prices automatically.

Everything from *Normalize* through *Prioritize* is **pure, deterministic, and
versioned**, so the company's judgment is testable and auditable. Only *Observe*
and *Execute* touch the outside world, and only behind adapters.

---

## 2. The company objective and the one currency

**Objective:** maximize expected company value. For an early-stage business this
is the risk- and time-adjusted **net present value of future cash flows** created
by an action — expressed as **ΔEV in dollars**.

Every action, regardless of department, is priced on the same axis via the **KPI
Value Graph** (§3). This is what makes "the CTO's onboarding fix" and "the CMO's
TikTok campaign" and "the CRO's pricing change" *directly comparable*. See
`docs/adr/ADR-002` for why this is dollars and why the three "impacts"
(revenue / retention / acquisition) are **partitions of one number, never a sum**.

---

## 3. The KPI Value Graph (how upstream movement becomes one dollar figure)

Model the funnel + monetization as a DAG. Each node is priced by how much
downstream subscriber revenue one incremental unit at that node produces:

```
P(subscribe | node) = (product of forward conversion rates from node → return_visit)
                      × free_to_paid_anchor
value(node)         = P(subscribe | node) × LTV
```

- The **free-to-paid rate** is the single monetization anchor (calibrated from
  real revenue data; Growth Science owns it). It replaces any toy funnel tail.
- **LTV** is the value of one net-new subscriber.

Because `value(node)` already contains *all* downstream conversion to a paying
subscriber, you value a change **at the one node it moves** and never re-add the
downstream steps. That is the anti-double-count mechanism.

### ΔEV per action

```
DURABLE rate fix (persists for every future cohort):
  ΔEV = monthlyPopulation(parent) × Δrate × value(childNode) × 12 × durability

ONE-SHOT bolus (a campaign injecting a batch of units once):
  ΔEV = units × value(node) × durability

MONETIZATION multiplier (pricing/packaging — moves the terminal conversion):
  ΔEV = monthlyPopulation(signup) × Δ P(subscribe|signup) × LTV × 12 × durability
```

Each Decision carries three cases — **conservative / base / optimistic** — plus a
separate `confidence`. The concrete, computed WatchVerdict ledger is in
`docs/DECISION_LEDGER_MOCK.md` and is generated by
`src/lib/decision-preview/*` (a labeled DEMO preview, test-locked).

### Guardrails and honesty
- A play that lifts one node but harms a guardrail KPI is valued **net of the
  guardrail damage** via the graph → Goodhart resistance is in the math.
- Option value (PR domain authority, channel-learning) is **excluded from ΔEV**
  and handled by the explore budget, so exploratory bets are not falsely ranked
  low *and* not falsely counted as revenue.
- The engine can return **"insufficient evidence"**: when a Signal cannot be
  bound to a KPI node or a play's base-rate has no prior, it is quarantined, not
  ranked.

## 4. The executive layer (accountability, not agents)

Executives are **not autonomous negotiating agents.** Each is three data fields:
a **mandate** (a cluster of KPI nodes), a **team** (sensors + executors), and a
**scorecard** (did their funded proposals deliver predicted ΔEV?). They *propose*
and *own outcomes*; they never set priority. See `docs/adr/ADR-003`.

| Seat | KPI mandate | Live in Phase 2? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CEO | company value (root) + allocation + founder-in-loop | yes (the allocator) |
| CGO Growth | qualified visitors → signup, referral/virality | yes |
| CPO Product | signup → DNA → first verdict → successful rec (**WV bottleneck**) | yes |
| CDS Data Science | value model, attribution, experiments, calibration | yes |
| CFO | budget/capacity constraints, CAC:LTV, cost ceilings | yes (as constraints) |
| CMO Marketing | impression→click→landing; SEO/social/creator/paid/PR | interface |
| CRO Revenue | subscription, pricing, trial conversion | interface (activates near RV launch) |
| CTO Tech | reliability, latency, cost-to-serve, eng capacity supply | interface |
| CCO Customer | return_visit, churn, NPS | interface |
| CPRO / CPartO | PR + partnership nodes (episodic, option-value) | interface |

Staff four, stub the rest. Capability for 11; headcount for 4.

**Budget competition = a capital market.** Each cycle, a resource pool (cash,
capacity, founder attention, risk, reputation) is allocated by one constrained
optimizer maximizing Σ **TotalValue** (§12, not just ΔEV). Outcomes update each
seat's **credibility weight** (calibration-adjusted); chronic over-promisers get
their future estimates shrunk toward priors. Nobody argues — the track record
reprices proposals.

**Responsibility deltas from the value-function additions (§9–§12):**
- **CDS (Data Science)** owns learning-value/EVOI estimation, belief
  recalibration, and the calibration curve (are our confidences honest?).
- **CFO** owns the constraint budgets — including **founder attention** and the
  **irreversible-risk cap** — and the explore quota's cash line.
- **CEO** owns the composite-objective weights and risk appetite (set with CDS),
  and assigns each decision's **class** (ADR-005) and reversibility bar.
- **Every seat's scorecard** now also grades calibration of its confidence and
  reversibility estimates, not just realized ΔEV.

## 5. Growth Science (the loop that improves the engine)

The meta-department. It never emits one-off recommendations; it changes the
priors the engine reasons with:

- **Experiment Ledger** — every test with hypothesis, target KPI node, guardrail,
  result, and a verdict enum `worked | failed | inconclusive | never_repeat`
  (durable negative knowledge — the thing startups keep re-burning money on).
- **Attribution** — separates real lift from noise/seasonality (holdouts,
  pre/post with control).
- **Belief/Prior store** — recalibrates KPI-graph edges, LTV, per-channel CAC,
  and play base-rates. Versioned, never silently mutated.
- **Calibration & Exploration** — measures the engine's own accuracy (are
  70%-confidence calls right ~70% of the time?) and owns the **explore budget**
  (value-of-information bets like the Meta paid test).

It also reports on the OS itself: **decision yield**, **calibration**, and
**loop latency** (observe→learn cycle time) — the single most important metric of
the system at this stage.

## 6. Event model & agent communication

**Actors over an append-only event log**, not RPC:

- The `events` table is the source of truth. Observations, decisions, approvals,
  executions, measurements, belief updates are all events. Audit, replay, and
  idempotency come free.
- **Contract = versioned typed envelopes** (`type` + `version`). Producers and
  consumers decouple; add a consumer without touching producers.
- Workers are **stateless and idempotent** (keyed by event id / idempotency key).
  Ordering is per-aggregate, not global.
- v1 transport: Postgres `events` + a transactional **outbox** + poller. Later:
  swap the drain for a real queue — no producer/consumer changes.
- This is why the design scales to thousands of specialists: coordination is the
  ledger, and adding a worker is registering a new subscriber under an existing
  executive mandate.

## 7. Safety invariants (never relax)

1. Approvals are **default-deny**; unknown/externally-visible actions are gated.
2. No public, financial, deployment, pricing, or outreach action bypasses approval.
3. Every automated action creates an **audit event**.
4. Every decision retains **evidence, provenance, assumptions, uncertainty, and
   audit history**.
5. The system can say **"insufficient evidence"** and can **kill an initiative**
   after poor measured results (a `kill` decision that reverses or halts it).
6. Product separation (WV vs RV) is never removed.
7. Secrets are server-only; no public unauthenticated deployment.
8. Seed/mock/preview data is unmistakably labeled DEMO and never presented as real.

---

## 8. What this deliberately does NOT build yet

No thousand-agent mesh, no live providers, no async execution, no auto-posting,
no money movement. The moat is the **calibrated value model + the event ledger +
the belief store** — those are cheap to build now and make agents cheap to add
later. Everything else is deferred to the roadmap in `docs/ROADMAP.md` and
sequenced in `docs/PHASE_2_MIGRATION_PLAN.md`.

## 9. Decision reversibility (ADR-004)

Every Decision carries a reversibility level — `easy_to_reverse`, `moderate_cost`,
`difficult`, `nearly_irreversible` — backed by a continuous `cost_to_reverse`.
Reversibility changes the ranking three ways:

```
downsideRisk = P(bad_outcome) × cost_to_reverse
riskAdjEV    = EV_base × confidence − downsideRisk          (irreversible costs more)
optionValue  = uncertainty × upsideRetained × (1 − reverseFriction)
               (reversible + uncertain = a cheap call option → positive)
```

Plus a **portfolio cap on concurrent one-way doors** (risk budget). Net effect:
between two equal-EV actions, the more reversible one wins; reversible bets clear a
lower evidence bar, so the company runs more of them and learns faster.

## 10. Decision classes (ADR-005)

Every Decision is tagged **A (strategic) · B (product) · C (growth experiment) ·
D (learning investment)**. Class is largely implied by reversibility + primary value
source, and each class has a default governance policy (approval, evidence bar,
acceptable uncertainty, review cadence, rollback expectation, measurement period)
stored in `decision_class_policies`. Governance is proportional: founder attention
goes to A/B; C/D run fast on thin evidence. Default-deny still holds — classes set
*how much* process, never *whether* an externally-visible action needs approval.

## 11. Learning value (ADR-006)

Learning value is **expected value of information, in dollars**:

```
LearningValue = Σ_{future decisions f that depend on what this reveals}
                  P(result flips f) × |ΔEV swing of f| × time_discount
```

It only counts if it would **change a future decision** — interesting-but-inert
information scores zero. Class D decisions are ranked primarily on it; a negative
direct ΔEV is acceptable when learning value clears the cost (the Meta paid test).
Growth Science recalibrates versioned belief distributions after each experiment
(prior → measure → Bayesian posterior → re-price ledger → update base-rates, class
priors, exec credibility, and the calibration curve).

## 12. The CEO portfolio — maximize long-term value, not EV

```
maximize  Σ TotalValue(d),  TotalValue = riskAdjEV + LearningValue + optionValue + strategicValue
subject to:  cash ≤ budget · eng_days ≤ capacity · founder_attention ≤ capacity
             · irreversible_risk ≤ risk budget · dependencies · funnel diversification
             · RESERVE an explore quota for Class C/D · blend short/long horizon
```

The explore reserve, the learning-value term, and strategic/durability weighting are
what turn "maximize EV" into "maximize long-term company value." **Implementation is
one composite scalar + three hard constraints + one reserve — not a Pareto solver**
(§13). Weights are Growth-Science-tunable, never frozen constants.

## 13. Freeze recommendation

**Recommendation: FREEZE the operating model now, with two riders.**

Freeze the *structure* — the pipeline, the Decision envelope (EV, confidence, risk,
reversibility, class, learning value), the four classes, the composite objective and
its constraint set, the event/belief substrate, and the safety invariants (§7). This
model has converged: the last three additions are refinements of the *value function*
and *governance metadata*, not new architecture, and each slots into the existing
envelope and portfolio selector.

- **Rider 1 — freeze structure, not numbers.** All coefficients (risk penalties,
  learning-value and strategic weights, class thresholds, reversibility costs) are
  Growth-Science-tunable parameters in `beliefs`, not frozen constants.
- **Rider 2 — freeze only what Phase 2 needs.** The multi-product allocator, async
  execution, and the additional executive seats stay explicitly out of the freeze
  and deferred to the roadmap.

Further pre-implementation design would now be speculative — itself a form of
over-engineering. The highest-value next act is to **build Phase 2 steps 1–2**
(event log + belief store, then calibrate the KPI value graph on WV's real funnel)
and let measured results, not more design, drive the next changes.
